    References and Further Reading¶

    This page lists primary sources, standards documents, historical accounts, and academic papers consulted during the development of this COBOL reference documentation.

    Primary Standards and Specifications¶

    1. CODASYL, "COBOL - Initial Specifications for a COmmon Business Oriented Language," Government Printing Office, April 1960.
    2. CODASYL, "COBOL-1961 Revised Specifications for a COmmon Business Oriented Language," Government Printing Office, August 1961.
    3. CODASYL, "COBOL - Edition 1965," Conference on Data Systems Languages, 1965.
    4. CODASYL COBOL Committee, "COBOL - Journal of Development 1968," NBS Handbook 106, National Bureau of Standards, July 1969. 344 pages.
    5. ANSI, "American National Standard COBOL, X3.23-1968," American National Standards Institute, 1968. First ANSI standard.
    6. ANSI, "American National Standard COBOL, X3.23-1974," American National Standards Institute, 1974. Second ANSI standard.
    7. ANSI/ISO, "American National Standard for Information Systems - Programming Language - COBOL, X3.23-1985 / ISO 1989:1985," 1985. Third standard, 824 pages, 11 modules.
    8. ISO/IEC 1989:2002, "Information technology - Programming languages - COBOL," International Organization for Standardization, 2002.
    9. ISO/IEC 1989:2014, "Information technology - Programming languages, their environments and system software interfaces - Programming language COBOL," International Organization for Standardization, 2014.
    10. ISO/IEC 1989:2023, "Information technology - Programming languages, their environments and system software interfaces - Programming language COBOL," International Organization for Standardization, 2023.
